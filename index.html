The Problem of Food Waste

Food waste is a serious global problem that costs both money and negatively impacts our planet. In the EU alone, 88 million tons of food are wasted every year.

Consumer Causes

Meal planning errors, large portions, impulse purchases, and misunderstood date labeling are some of the biggest causes.

Business Causes

Poor storage and overproduction in supermarkets often lead to food waste that could have been consumed.

Ethical Challenge

While millions of tons of food are thrown away, millions of people suffer from hunger. This is a major moral challenge.

Consequences of Food Waste

Food waste causes environmental and economic problems that we can no longer ignore. Every kilogram of food that goes to waste releases CO2 and contributes to climate change.

Solutions to Food Waste

There are several ways we can reduce food waste, both as individuals and as a community. Here are some of the most important actions:

Donate Food

Donate excess food to charity to help those in need while reducing waste.

Donate Food

Sustainable Production

Companies and farmers should use modern storage techniques to reduce food waste from production to sale.

Changed Consumer Habits

Educating consumers about shopping planning and proper storage can significantly reduce food waste at home.

Food Waste Statistics

Every year, 88 million tons of food are wasted in the EU alone. On a global scale, that number is even higher. Food waste costs millions and has a catastrophic impact on our environment.
